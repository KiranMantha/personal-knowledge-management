Over the last few months I’ve been trading pretty actively on **Polymarket**, and one thing kept bothering me:

Press enter or click to view image in full size

![]()

You can see *that* a market is moving, but you can’t see *why*.

Sometimes the move starts from an X post.  
Sometimes it comes from Bloomberg or Politico.  
Sometimes it’s a tiny Telegram channel with 2,000 followers that somehow publishes information before anyone else notices.

After getting burned a few times by moves I didn’t catch early enough, I decided to build a tool for myself — now called [**Alphascope**](https://www.alphascope.app/) — that would solve this problem. <https://www.alphascope.app/>

It ended up doing three core things:

## 1️⃣ Paste any Polymarket link → instantly pull every relevant source

[Alphascope](https://www.alphascope.app/) scans the entire news universe around that market:

* X / Twitter posts
* Bloomberg, Business outlets, Politico
* Niche Telegram channels
* Smaller blogs and emerging news feeds

Basically, anything that can move a market — fast or slow.

## 2️⃣ Everything is grouped into one timeline with clear signals

Instead of digging through fragmented sources, you get a unified view:

* where the move originated
* which sources contributed
* what narrative actually caused the shift
* what’s important vs what’s just noise

It’s the closest thing I’ve found to “market x-ray vision.”

## 3️⃣ Full reasoning transparency

I didn’t want a black-box LLM giving me vibes.  
 I wanted **auditability**.

So the tool shows:

* every source the system used
* why each source was included
* how it influenced the final forecast or narrative detection

You can literally open the model’s full reasoning chain.

## Why I’m sharing this

I originally built [Alphascope](https://www.alphascope.app/) just so I wouldn’t miss early signals again.  
But after showing it to a few traders, a lot of them asked me to make it public — so I put together an early demo

If you’re deep into **Polymarket** or prediction markets in general, I’d genuinely love feedback:

* What signals matter most to you?
* What would help you catch narrative shifts earlier?
* What pain points should I solve next?

I’m happy to answer questions or share examples.
