### Day 1 - Where to Start?

Be it any exam or any new thing for which I have to start learning, it’s very important for me to understand the main contents/objectives within that area which we can consider as a syllabus.

This [link](https://d1.awsstatic.com/training-and-certification/docs-ai-practitioner/AWS-Certified-AI-Practitioner_Exam-Guide.pdf) from official AWS certification helped me to understand each and every aspect of the exam starting from *whom this exam for* to *what are the objectives and score content for this certification*.

I strongly recommend you to go through this first, before you start referring to any other course content. It helps you to understand the exam motive and content on a high level basis.

### Day 2 - Practice Exam before Learning Resources !

After understanding the contents for the exam, I felt like a lot of things mentioned in that link were already familiar to me as I studied few things about AI/ML during my college.

Hence, before I started going through any course content, I decided to take a sample practice test to understand my strengths and weaknesses.

As I heard there is free sample test with 65 questions on AWS Skill Builder, I went for it.

### Day 5 - Practice Tests from Udemy

I bought a set of 4 practice exam questions from [Stephane Maarek’s](https://www.udemy.com/course/practice-exams-aws-certified-ai-practitioner/) Udemy course. I did not go through the actual course as I was informed by my friends that it was very vast and in depth.

Also, I was already pretty familiar with the syllabus and concepts. However, you can go through it if you have time.

According to my preparation, I thought I could easily pass the practice tests. But it was a bummer when I took the first practice test as I was able to score only 69%!
